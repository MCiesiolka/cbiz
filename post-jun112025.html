Are You Showing Up in Search? A Beginner’s Guide to Local SEO for Small Biz

Published: June 11, 2025

You don’t have to be a tech wizard to show up on Google. But if your business isn’t popping up when someone nearby searches for what you offer… you’re losing money.

What Is Local SEO (and Why Should You Care)?

Local SEO means setting up your online presence so people in your area can find you when they search for services like yours. Think:

  • 🗣 “balloon arch near me”
  • 🗣 “Marion event decorators”
  • 🗣 “party rentals in Columbus IN”

If your business isn’t coming up in those results—or if someone has to dig to find you—you’re leaving leads on the table.

Here’s How to Show Up Locally—Without Getting Overwhelmed:

1. Claim and Complete Your Google Business Profile

This is the most important step. It’s free and shows up right in search results and Google Maps.

📌 Make sure your:

  • ✔️ Business name, hours, and contact info are correct
  • ✔️ Categories match what you do (e.g., “Event Planner,” “Party Equipment Rental”)
  • ✔️ Photos are up to date
  • ✔️ Reviews are turned ON—and you reply to them!

✅ Visit: google.com/business to claim or update your listing.

2. Make Sure Your Info Matches Across the Web

Your name, address, phone number, and website (aka “NAP”) should look exactly the same on every platform: Facebook, Instagram, Yelp, Peerspace, GigSalad—you name it.

❌ “123 Main St.” on one and “123 Main Street” on another? That inconsistency can hurt your SEO.

3. Add Local Keywords to Your Website and Posts

Use the name of your city or service area in your content. If you’re in Columbus, IN —say it!

💬 Examples:

  • “We provide baby shower backdrops across Bartholomew County.”
  • “Serving events all over Southwest Indiana with custom draping and design.”

🧠 Bootcamp Tip: The more often your content includes local terms, the more Google connects you to local searches.

4. Encourage Reviews (and Respond to Them!)

Positive reviews build trust and boost your visibility in search. Ask happy clients to leave a review, and always respond—especially to the good ones!

📲 Example reply:

“Thanks so much for the kind words! We loved being part of your event 💛”

5. Post Regularly to Your Google Business Profile

Yup—just like social media. Google lets you post updates, photos, and events right on your profile. Doing this shows you’re active and helps you rank higher.

📆 Tip: Reuse content you’re already posting on Facebook or Instagram. No need to reinvent the wheel!

Final Thoughts

Local SEO might sound technical, but it’s really about being findable. If your ideal clients are nearby and searching for what you offer—you should be the one they find.
